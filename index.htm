Three basic steps to prolong the life of flowers

Keeping your cut flowers fresh for an extra few days may be challenging, but quite possible. Below find a few easy and basic steps to prolong their life.

1

Pick a clean vase

Never skip step number one. Picking a clean vase is as essential as watering your flowers. So to keep the flowers fresh and attractive, first clean your vase to eliminate harmful bacteria. Scrub it in bleach and water, then rinse thoroughly.

2

Feed the flowers

Yes, yes! Flowers need to be fed in order to survive. They need sugar for nourishment and an acidic ingredient, such as aspirin, to help them absorb water. You can buy ready-made food or make your own with sugar, bleach, and lime juice.

3

Refresh the water

Flowers die because of the lack of water. Even a few minutes without water can cause the stems to dry out. Check the water level frequently to make sure stem ends are generously covered. Every five days, change the water completely.
